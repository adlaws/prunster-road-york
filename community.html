The Community

York Township

Welcome to the township of York, Western Australia and its friendly, relaxed community. With its well-preserved 19th century architecture, York is full of beautiful old buildings. There are great cafes, bustling hotel-bars and popular bakeries selling classic Aussie meat pies — you’ll have to debate with the locals which bakery has the best ones…

Arts & Culture

York is a popular tourist destination, showcasing enticing giftwares and crafts, a vintage and classic cars Motor Museum, a Residency Museum, beautiful historical buildings and four Heritage Walk Trails.

Throughout the year it hosts delights such as the Brockwell Classic Car Run, York Agricultural Show, a Jazz Festival, a Garden Festival, the York Art and Craft Awards and York Cup horse race.

York Festival

For two weeks each year York welcomes visitors to the community-run York Festival, a delightful celebration of Australian culture and fun. From the organisers:

“ The York Festival attracts thousands of people over September and October to WA’s oldest inland town. Our location in the West Australian Wheatbelt informs much of the content of our program, the Wara Art Trail spectacular straw sculptures, the Running of the Lambs costumed obstacle event, the The York Maze designed by local school kids, and the entrepreneurial Tips from the Richest Man in the Colony! Other highlights include Chalk The Walk pavement art, York Art & Craft Awards (since 1973), the Medieval Fayre living history, From Hooves to Highways photographic exhibition, a Day in Japan extravaganza of Japanese culture, the York Open Garden Art Trail plus walking tours, art, comedy, live music and heaps more! Guaranteed to add the wow factor this year is the creation of four giant straw sculptures by Japanese artist Akira Moriya. Watch, or get involved in the construction of these spectacular representations of endangered Australian creatures. ”

Your Community

Plus the outskirts of town offer the York Motocross Track, York Horse Racing, the York Ice-Creamery located at York Olive Oil, the York Recreation and Convention Centre, heritage walk trails and a playground by the river.
